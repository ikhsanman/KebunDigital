It just keeps falling. Drop by drop. Red on the concrete. It’s almost funny, isn't it? My body is quietly falling apart while I’m just trying to figure out how to breathe. People always tell you to look up. To face forward. But they don’t understand the gravity of it. The past isn't just something that happened *to* me, it’s a physical weight. It’s this colossal, rotting anchor dragging my neck down. 

Every mistake, every betrayal, every single ruinous thing I’ve lived through, it’s all trapped right here, echoing in my skull. A museum of wreckage I’m forced to wander through every time I blink. It’s so heavy. But if I manage to tear my eyes away from what was, I’m immediately blinded by what *could* be. Tomorrow. The sheer, terrifying vastness of it. 

A million diverging paths, and in my mind, every single one of them ends in a tragedy. The "what ifs" swarm around me like insects. What if I fail again? What if it gets worse? What if I'm fundamentally broken and the rest of my life is just me waiting for the other shoe to drop? It’s an absurd, endless abyss of possibilities, and looking into it makes the air feel so painfully thin. 

I want to move. I swear I do. I want to wipe this blood off my face, stand up straight, and just... begin again. I want to rebel against this script. I want to take a single, defiant step forward. But I can't. There is this crushing pressure, right here in the center of my chest. Like I’ve swallowed a stone. And my head... it’s throbbing with this dense, suffocating fog that refuses to lift. It holds me completely paralyzed in the present, sandwiched perfectly between the ghosts of yesterday and the terror of tomorrow. I am suffocating on air.

So, I just stand here. Head bowed. Waiting. Hoping, perhaps, that if I stand still long enough, the heaviness will just bleed out with the rest of it.
